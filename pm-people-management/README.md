# pm-people-management

People management skills for Product Managers. Covers the full people lifecycle: staffing plans, hiring, onboarding, performance management, career growth, team health, and succession planning.

## Skills (12)

- **team-staffing-plan** — Build a headcount plan: roles needed, timing, budget, and justification aligned to product roadmap.
- **team-capability-assessment** — Assess current team skills against what the roadmap requires, identify gaps and upskilling needs.
- **succession-planning** — Identify key-person risks and develop succession plans for critical roles.
- **job-description** — Write compelling, structured job descriptions that attract the right candidates.
- **interview-kit** — Design competency-based interview kits with scorecards, questions, and evaluation criteria.
- **hiring-scorecard** — Create structured hiring rubrics to evaluate candidates consistently and reduce bias.
- **candidate-evaluation** — Compare and evaluate candidates post-interview with structured analysis and recommendations.
- **performance-review** — Write effective performance reviews with evidence-based feedback and growth-oriented framing.
- **career-ladder** — Design role leveling frameworks and career paths for product/tech teams.
- **onboarding-plan** — Create structured onboarding plans for new team members (30/60/90 day).
- **pip-plan** — Design fair, clear Performance Improvement Plans with measurable goals and support structures.
- **team-health-check** — Assess team morale, workload, collaboration, and psychological safety with actionable insights.

## Commands (4)

- `/pm-people-management:people-plan` — Team planning: staffing, capability assessment, and succession planning.
- `/pm-people-management:people-hire` — Hiring end-to-end: JD, interview design, evaluation, and decision.
- `/pm-people-management:people-grow` — People development: performance reviews, career paths, onboarding, and PIPs.
- `/pm-people-management:people-health` — Team health check: morale, workload, collaboration, and psychological safety.
